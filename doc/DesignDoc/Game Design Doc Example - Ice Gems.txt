Game Title: Ice Gems 


EXECUTIVESUMMARY 


Ice Gems is an action-packed, exploration game that allows anyone to enter the beautiful 
ice caves of the mystical Himalaya Mountains. You take on the role of a futuristic 
mountain-climber who’s on a quest to collect all 10 magical gems located in the different ice 
caves found in the monstrous rock walls of the mountains. However, your ice shoes plummeted 
down the mountain on your ascent up to the peak, so you must use the propulsion from your 
jet-pack backpack to glide your way through the super slippery cave floors. 


With a limited amount of resources, huge ice pillars and walls barricading your paths, 
freezing cold ice ponds, as well as a dangerous abominable snowman in the cave, our game asks 
you to strategically collect mystical gems to fuel your jetpack to journey to the back of the cave 
where the magical gem of that level is located. 


We believe that this game uniquely challenges players’ hand-eye coordination, visual 
perception, and understanding of physics to successfully capture all of the gems in the 
mountains. The simple and maze-like level structure allows for quick, fun-fueled gameplay and 
an ability to increase your skill-ability with each gameplay. 


The game is developed purely for PC platforms. We expect end-to-end implementation to 
take 5 weeks or, assuming 3 part-time designers/developers 14 man-hours.

2.2.1.1 
Player 
goals 
and

Main goal is to get to the end of the cave and retrieve the special gem. 
Secondary goal is to collect gems as your resources go through the cave. 


Type of challenges includes obstacles. Ice poles and ice walls block your way, 
and ice puddles cost your gems if you accidentally slide into them. In addition, the 
abominable snowman roars occasionally, which moves you backward unless you hide 
behind a ice pole or a ice wall. 


Types of conflicts include resource management. You can propel yourself 
forward to reach more gems but in doing so you also use gems your own. 
Winning condition – you need to defeat the abominable snowman and retrieve the 
special gem. 


2.2.1.2 
Main 
rules 
and 
procedures 
Operational rules – You can collect gems on the wall of the cave, and you could slide 
on the ice using your jet-pack. The more fuel you use to propel yourself, the faster you 
move and the more gems you use. You can shoot rubies at the abominable snowman to 
melt it down in order to retrieve the crystal at the end and win the game. 


Main game mechanic – The character will look at the direction of the mouse, and the 
mouse click and hold determines how much fuel the character uses (holding the mouse 
click for longer indicates more fuel use). The player can throw rubies at the abominable 
snowman by pressing the E key. The player can collect gems by pressing F. The player 
can fly up using the spacebar. 


Player Resources 


Types of resources: Ruby, Diamond 


How does the player use resources to achieve goals? 
Rubies can be used to melt down ice, which is useful in defeating the abominable 
snowman. Every time a ruby hits the abominable snowman, the abominable snowman 
decreases in size. Diamonds are used to propel yourself forward. 


2.2.1.3 
Boundaries 
and 
Constraints 
The actions are limited to the empty areas within the cave. The player cannot climb onto 
ice walls or ice poles. The player can only collect gems within reach, which will light up 
in the gameplay. The player cannot bypass the abominable snowman before melting it 
down.

3.1.1 
Hardware 
The game will run on Mac OS X and Windows 64-bit computers with quad-core Intel or 
AMD processors, 2.5 GHz or faster, and 8 GB RAM. System should have NVIDIA 
GeForce 470 GTX or AMD Radeon 6870 HD series graphics card or higher. 


3.1.2 
Software 
We will use Unreal Engine 4 to develop the game. It will be available for Windows 10 
and Mac OS X 10.9.2 or later. 


3.1.3 
Algorithms 
and 
Techniques 
Algorithms: 


● 
Player velocity: Since the game is heavily inertia-based, the current velocity the 
player is moving is extremely important. Velocity will be tracked as a Vec3 of 
floats: when the player hits a certain key, the jetpack will apply a positive 
acceleration in the direction of the player’s forward vector (with upwards 
component normalized and removed). If the player wishes to fly upward, a 
different key can be pressed to move the player in the direction of the look vector 
without the upward component removed. When the player moves forward, the 
friction of the ice applies a negative force on the player’s velocity (the player 
slows down as they skate on the ice longer), and when flying upward, gravity is 
pulling the player back down. Crashing into a wall or pillar in the environment 
will reverse the direction of the inertia (based on physics momentum). 
● 
Gem collection: When a gem is within the reach of the player (the gem position is 
within a certain radius of the player’s position), the gem will glow, indicating the 
player can collect it. Clicking “F” will collect the fetch the gem and add it to the 
player’s inventory (seen in fuel bar and ruby bar). 
● 
Fuel bar update: Throughout the game, you collect gems to power your jetpack. 
Whenever you use the jetpack, the fuel bar decreases, and whenever you collect 
gems, the fuel bar increases. We will track the fuel level using a float variable, 
increasing and decreasing accordingly with key presses and gem acquisitions. If 
the fuel level hits 0 and the player’s velocity also hits 0 (or almost 0), the player 
loses. 
● 
Special gem collection bar: In the game, there are special ruby gems that are 
needed to defeat the boss at the end of the game. There is a status collection bar 
for this as well showing the percentage of rubies you’ve acquired so far out of all 
the rubies available in the level. This is calculated as a float based on an integer 
7 



variable representing the number of rubies currently collected and the total 
number of rubies available. 


● 
Monster roars: There is a roar that echoes through the cave at set intervals which 
applies a force to the player in the opposite direction of the goal. This roar echoes 
through the cave and increases in volume as the “echo” gets closer to the player— 
this gives the player time to prepare for gust of wind from the roar as it 
approaches. Depending on the player’s current position in the cave, we will have 
a series of different audio recordings (of louder and louder roars) to play as the 
player gets closer to the source of the roars, and decreasing time intervals set for 
the player to react to the roar. 
● 
Player obstacles: If a player hides behind an ice pillar (this can be determined by 
if the player’s position is within a certain range of an ice pillar’s), the ice pillar 
blocks the force of the monster’s roar from the player and the player does not get 
pushed backwards. The cave also has cracks / puddles in the ice, and if the player 
falls into one of these, they will lose 70% of their gems. 
● 
Door busting: If the player hits a door with a large enough velocity, the door will 
shatter. Otherwise, the player will react as if just hitting a regular obstacle 
(cavewall, ice pillar). 
● 
Boss battle: To enter the boss battle, the player clicks on the boss in the final 
cavern, and the third person camera mode switches into first person. The boss 
battle comprises of a few algorithms. 
○ 
Follow algorithm: The “boss” in the final room will follow the player, 
blocking them from the final goal. The boss’ position will update based on 
the position of the player. 
○ 
Throwing rubies: The player can hit a key to throw rubies at the ice 
monster. This will apply a force in the direction of the player’s look vector 
to a ruby. 
○ 
Shrinking: If a ruby hits the boss, he begins to “melt” and decreases in 
scale by a certain amount. To do this, we will have a model for the 
monster and simply decrease the model in size every time a ruby hits, or 
replace this model with a smaller “melting” version of the current model.

The player starts the game by choosing a level. Once the level is selected, the player 
enters playing mode. The player can pause at any point and resume, quit, or restart. At 
any point, if the player runs out of both fuel and inertia or fails to beat the boss, the game 
is lost. Otherwise, successfully reaching the end and beating the boss results in a win. 
Later levels can only be reached after earlier levels are beaten.

4.2.2 
Major 
Alpha 
Version 


The alpha version should just have the core mechanics of the game - namely, the player 
should be able to propel themselves through a simple environment while collecting gems. 
The player should lose a percentage of their collected gems if they enter an ice pond, 
should not be able to walk through the cave walls or pillars, and should be able to “melt” 
the abominable monster and collect the goal-driven gem at the end of the level. 


Beta 
Versions 


The beta version should have more complex mechanics in the game. Through playtesting 
we will include a fuel monitor which will calculate the fuel used and gained from the 
gems in the cave. There will be the introduction of major 3d assets to transport the player 
into the ice cave. There will be some basic animation and audio.
